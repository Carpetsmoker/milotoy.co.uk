About

Milo toy keeps young children in touch with an absent loved one

Using two way video messages and a treasure hunt game. It’s fun, engaging and a totally interactive experience for the whole family.

Mission

To improve relationships through play and communication

The most important way to sustain important family relationships is to encourage better and more regular communication.

Story

The need behind Milo has always been personal. My Dad served in the Army his whole life and most of my childhood was spent apart from him. He’d miss parents evening or events and it was never the same without him there.

At University I studied Industrial Designed and in final year we were charged with the task to design a product based on a real need. To me the problem of an absent parent was quick to spring to mind. After a long year of research and designing little Milo was born.

Gadget show live
highly commended 2016

We were invited to this year’s Gadget Show Live at the NEC in Birmingham. Milo was exhibited in the British Inventor’s Project (BIP) which aims to celebrate innovation and showcase new ideas. The project has been running since 2014 and has only been growing in strength.

We won our spot after pitching to eight toy companies at the Toy News Inventor’s Workshop last year. I had about ten minutes to pitch to the professionals in a Dragon’s Den style event. It was a nerve­wracking but rewarding experience as Milo was shown off to the toy industry.
